Aivvy

Aivvy makes smart headphones which learn your music tastes and stream personalized music for you. The headphones feature a built-in music player powered by a recommendation algorithm that adapts to your listening behavior — no phone required, no subscription necessary.

Role

As a UX intern, I was responsible for designing the Aivvy companion app from end to end — covering wireframes, interaction design, and high-fidelity prototypes. I collaborated closely with the founding team to define the core user experience for pairing, personalization, and music playback control.

I also planned and facilitated usability testing sessions to validate the out-of-box setup experience, hardware LED feedback, and core app flows. Tools used included Sketch and InVision.

Research

We recruited participants across two rounds of moderated usability testing, targeting early adopters interested in wearables and music streaming. Each session included task-based scenarios followed by open-ended feedback.

Our three main research goals were:

  1. Evaluate the open-box and first-time setup experience of the headphones
  2. Determine whether users could interpret the LED status indicators without prior instruction
  3. Assess the usability and learnability of the companion app

A key finding from testing was that users struggled to interpret LED status indicators — the colors and blink patterns were not intuitive without a reference guide. To address this, we introduced a voice prompt system that announces device status aloud at key moments (powering on, pairing, low battery), eliminating the need to memorize LED patterns.

Aivvy was successfully funded on Kickstarter and proudly awarded by Red Dot Design Award and CES Innovation Awards.

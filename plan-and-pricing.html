Plan and Pricing

Overview

I re-designed the plan and pricing purchase experience for Docusign, which eventually drove 8% of conversion and 29% of ASP increase.

YEAR

2025

TIMELINE

1 month

COMPANY

DOCUSIGN

Goals

The primary goal is to redesign the pricing page to align with the new rebrand and differentiate it from our original e-Signature offerings. Eventually unlock new customer conversions.

Target Audience

Individuals interested in contract lifecycle solutions and eSignature products.

In 2024, DocuSign announced a new rebrand and introduced Intelligent Agreement Management (IAM), a powerful new platform. IAM integrates artificial intelligence (AI) and automation throughout the agreement lifecycle.

The IAM platform introduces key components:

  • Docusign Navigator – An AI-powered repository that centralizes agreements' storage, management, and analysis, converting unstructured documents into structured, actionable data.
  • Docusign Maestro – A no-code workflow engine that enables users to build customized agreement workflows, automating tasks such as document generation, approvals, and notifications—without writing a single line of code.

When the new platform was introduced, the team added an "IAM" tab to the right side in the original design to understand how customers react to the new offerings without cannibalizing our original e-Signature solution.

We realized people aren't fully understanding the IAM offerings, with less than 10% of people actually clicking on the view all features section (see below).

To fix the usability problems on the pricing page, I originally proposed the following design as a separate experiment.

Here is some feedback from the usability testing:

  • Iconography helps users locate the features they are looking for more easily.
  • By highlighting the "View All" features section, users can easily compare different plans and make quicker purchasing decisions.
  • Navigation can be further refined to better showcase the various plan types.

I led a few rounds of workshops and explorations below to iterate on different IA and components, working with the leadership and branding team.

After rounds of iterations and reviews, we finally settled on the following design with the key design intentions:

  • Action-oriented content to drive conversion
  • Card-type structure with focus on feature comparison
  • Value proposition for new IAM product
  • Surfacing view all feature for quick plan type comparison features

To understand the impact of the higher price point and premium product, the team ran an A/B/C test with the intention to validate the new direction.

  • Variant A: Original design with IAM tab (Default to eSignature tab)
  • Variant B: New design defaulting to eSignature tab (Lower price point)
  • Variant C: New design defaulting to IAM tab (Premium price point)

The Variant B new design is driving a 8% increase in conversion itself, but we noticed that Variant C results in a lower conversion but a 29% higher ASP. The design helped influence the company strategy to focus on conversion for self-serve customers, and IAM product for enterprise customers.
